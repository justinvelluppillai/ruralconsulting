Our People

Josh Brown

  •  Managing Director

Josh has a background in environmental consultancy and has worked across the fertiliser industry and with catchment groups. He is an expert in catchment group development and strategy, environmental project development, and agricultural environmental policy implementation. He has advised and works with farmers, Regional and Central government organisations, Catchment Groups, communities, and Iwi.

John Holmes

  •  Farm Consultant

Originally a farmer from Mid-Canterbury, John is passionate about New Zealand’s agricultural industry. With 25 years farming experience, coupled with industry roles with Beef + Lamb New Zealand and Ravensdown (Environmental), John a Certified Nutrient Management advisor helps farmers navigate the changing regulatory landscape and develop farm plans while making their businesses and communities more robust and sustainable.

Harry Millar

  •  Farm Consultant

With a diverse background, growing up in the high country, working as a fencer and as a land management advisor for ECAN, Harry has an empathy and unique ability to work with farmers to develop integrated solutions that combine/mesh environmental requirements with farm management and business development. He has skills in biodiversity enhancement, Emissions Trading Scheme (ETS) advice, environmental policy, and farm production planning and analysis. Harry is a Certified Nutrient Management advisor, and Farmax elite accredited user. (Harry grew up on a high-country station up the Ashburton Gorge before moving to a Sheep, Beef and Deer property in Peel Forest. With an academic background in Geography, Geology and Environmental Management, Harry now enjoys combining this with practical on-farm solutions to achieve the best all round outcome for the farm’s natural resources and economic viability. Previous roles include time spent in the rural fencing industry, a year on the Banks Peninsula as a Land Management Advisor with Environment Canterbury and now as a Catchment Farm Advisor with the Hurunui District Landcare Group (HDLG). These roles have given him a wide variety of experience across diverse farm systems, ranging from extensive high country to intensive dairy. No two farms are the same and it is this opportunity to understand the goals of each business and how best to support them that Harry enjoys the most. Always keen to talk about farming, weather and sport, feel free to get in touch.)

Ian Brown

  •  Principal Advisor

Ian has a long history in environmental and resource policy and management developed over his 50 year career working in catchment boards, regional councils and as a consultant. Ian is an expert in resource management, regional policy implementation, soil conservation, sustainable land use, farm environmental planning (including Freshwater Farm Plans), and community and iwi engagement. Ian, as one of the original creators of farm environment plans has contributed to their development as a tool for farmers and rural communities to see improved environmental outcomes over several decades.
